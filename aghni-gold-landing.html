Decision Guide

Gold Loan vs Selling Gold — Which Makes More Sense for You?

This is the most common question we receive. The fair answer depends on your situation — here is how to think it through clearly.

Gold Loan

When a Gold Loan Works

  • You need money temporarily and can repay within 6–12 months
  • The jewellery has deep sentimental or heirloom value
  • You believe gold prices will rise significantly before you repay
  • You pay 12–24% annual interest on most gold loans
  • Missing repayments puts your gold at auction risk
  • You only receive 65–75% of gold value as the loan amount
Selling Gold

When Selling Gold Makes More Sense

  • You need money now with no fixed plan to repay
  • The jewellery is unused, outdated, or damaged
  • You receive 90–98% of the gold market value — not a fraction
  • No interest, no EMI pressure, no risk of losing the asset to auction
  • You no longer own the physical gold after the sale
The honest take: If the gold is sitting unused in a locker and earning nothing, selling it and putting that money to work almost always comes out ahead of a gold loan — especially once you account for months of interest. That said, for jewellery with deep family significance, a short-term loan to manage a cash crunch is a completely reasonable choice. We help with both.

Rules & Documents

Can You Sell Gold Without a Bill in India?

Yes — and this surprises many people. You can legally sell gold without the original purchase invoice. India's gold selling rules do not require you to produce a receipt from when the jewellery was first bought.

What is required are basic identity documents to meet RBI's KYC requirements. Here is what to bring:

  • 🪪 Any one government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID)
  • 📋 PAN card required for transactions above ₹2 lakh (Income Tax Act)
  • 📸 A passport-size photograph may be needed for larger transactions
  • 💎 The gold itself — no original bill required
⚖️ Legal Clarity Worth Knowing

Selling inherited gold, received-as-gift gold, or gold bought without a bill is entirely legal in India. The Income Tax Act does not treat such sales as automatically taxable if the gold was held for more than three years (classified as a long-term capital asset). The tax treatment depends on your holding period and the source of the gold.

For amounts above ₹2 lakh, PAN is mandatory under law. We recommend consulting a CA for your specific situation. The above is general information, not legal or tax advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions About Selling Gold in Chennai — Answered Plainly

How is gold price calculated at Aghni Gold? ▼
We use the formula: Net Gold Weight × Purity Factor × Today's 24K Market Rate. Net weight means total weight minus any stones. Purity factor is your karat divided by 24 (for 22K, that is 22/24 = 0.916). Every step is shown on paper before you agree to anything.
Will I get the full gold rate shown online? ▼
You get the rate for your gold's actual purity. The 24K rate applies to 99.9% pure gold. If your jewellery is 22K (91.6% purity), your payout is 91.6% of the 24K rate — which is the correct and fair calculation. There are no hidden deductions beyond what is shown upfront.
Can I sell gold without the original bill or invoice? ▼
Yes. No original purchase bill is needed. You only need a government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID). For transactions above ₹2 lakh, PAN card is mandatory under Income Tax rules.
Is ID proof required to sell gold? ▼
Yes. Any one valid government-issued ID is required — this is an RBI compliance requirement, not something specific to us. It protects both the seller and buyer and keeps the transaction fully legal.
Do you buy broken or damaged gold jewellery? ▼
Absolutely. Broken, bent, scratched, or outdated design — none of that affects the gold value. We buy the metal, not the craftsmanship. Chains with missing clasps, rings with missing stones, old bangles — all accepted without any extra deduction.
How fast will I actually get paid? ▼
From the moment the purity test finishes to money in your hand — 15 minutes or less. Payment by cash (for smaller amounts), bank transfer, or UPI. For larger transactions, bank transfer is processed the same day.
What is 916 hallmark gold? ▼
916 means the gold contains 91.6% pure gold — this is 22 karat. The remaining 8.4% is usually copper or silver added for durability. Most Indian jewellery is 916 hallmarked and it is the most commonly traded karat in Chennai.
Can you take over my pledged gold from a bank or NBFC? ▼
Yes. We contact the lender directly, close the outstanding loan balance, collect the gold, test it, and pay you the value above the loan amount — usually on the same day you call us.

Will gold prices increase — should I sell now or wait? ▼
No one predicts gold prices reliably. What we can say is that selling when you need the money — rather than waiting to time the market perfectly — is almost always the right call.
